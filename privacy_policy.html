Privacy Policy for LeaveLedger

Effective Date: August 22, 2025

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how the LeaveLedger application ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information in connection with your use of the LeaveLedger web application (the "Service"). This Service is an official tool intended for use by authorized personnel of the Department of Education - Schools Division Office of the Island Garden City of Samal (DepEd SDO-IGaCoS) to manage employee leave credits efficiently and transparently.

2. Compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012

We are committed to upholding the rights of data subjects as mandated by Republic Act No. 10173, also known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012. All collection and processing of personal data under this Service are performed in adherence to the Act's core principles of transparency, legitimate purpose, and proportionality. The processing of your data is necessary for compliance with our legal and regulatory obligations as a government entity and for the performance of our public functions.

3. Information We Collect

We collect several types of information that are adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary to provide and improve our Service:

a. Account Information:

  • Full Name: To identify you within the system.
  • Official DepEd Email Address: Used as your unique username for login, for sending password reset links, and for official notifications.
  • User Role: Your designated role (e.g., "Teacher," "Standard Administrator," "System Administrator").
  • Assigned School/District: The specific school or district you are associated with.

b. Transactional Information:

  • Leave Credit Balance: Your current balance of available leave credits.
  • Leave Credit History: A permanent record of all transactions, including the amount, date, reason provided, and the name of the administrator who authorized the change.

c. User-Generated Content:

  • Feedback and Issue Reports: Information you voluntarily provide when you submit feedback or report a system issue.
  • Transaction Conversations: Messages exchanged between a teacher and an administrator regarding a specific leave transaction query.

d. Advertising and Analytics Data (Collected by Third Parties):

  • To support the Service, we display ads from Google. Our advertising partners may collect data using cookies and other identifiers. This may include your IP address, device identifiers, general location, and information about your browsing activity to serve relevant ads.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following specified and legitimate purposes:

  • To provide, maintain, and improve the core functionality of the Service.
  • To manage your account, authenticate you as a user, and ensure data security.
  • To maintain a transparent and permanent audit trail of all leave credit modifications.
  • To send important system notifications and respond to your inquiries and support requests.
  • For administrative reporting as required for official DepEd purposes.
  • To display advertisements through our partners (Google AdSense and AdMob) which help support the operational costs of the Service.

5. How We Share Your Information

Your information is shared strictly on a need-to-know basis:

  • Within DepEd SDO-IGaCoS: Information is shared with Administrators and authorized personnel as described in the original policy.
  • Third-Party Services: Your core data is securely stored using Google Firebase.
  • With Advertising Partners: We partner with Google to serve ads. Google uses data to personalize the ads you see. We do not share your personally identifiable account information (like your name, direct email, or specific leave balance) with our advertising partners. For more details, see Section 9 below.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law.

6. Data Security

We are committed to protecting your information. We implement reasonable and appropriate physical, technical, and organizational security measures to protect against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction of your personal data. You are also responsible for keeping your password confidential. Please do not share your login credentials with anyone.

7. Data Retention

Your personal and transactional information is considered an official record. It will be retained only for as long as necessary for the fulfillment of the purposes for which it was obtained, or for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims, or as provided by law.

8. Your Rights as a Data Subject

In accordance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012, you, as the data subject, are entitled to the following rights:

  • The Right to be Informed: You have the right to be informed whether personal data pertaining to you is being processed, as well as the scope and purpose of that processing.
  • The Right to Access: You have the right to reasonable access to your personal data upon demand.
  • The Right to Object: You have the right to object to the processing of your personal data.
  • The Right to Erasure or Blocking: You have the right to suspend, withdraw, or order the blocking, removal, or destruction of your personal data from our filing system.
  • The Right to Rectification: You have the right to dispute any inaccuracy or error in your personal data and have it corrected immediately, unless the request is vexatious or otherwise unreasonable.
  • The Right to Damages: You have the right to be indemnified for any damages sustained due to inaccurate, incomplete, outdated, false, unlawfully obtained, or unauthorized use of your personal data.
  • The Right to Data Portability: You have the right to obtain a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact your designated Standard Administrator or the System Administrator.

9. Cookies, Advertising, and Your Choices

Cookies: Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us and our partners recognize your browser or device.

Advertising: We use Google AdSense and Google AdMob to display advertisements on our Service.

  • Third-party vendors, including Google, use cookies (such as the Google advertising cookie) to serve ads based on your prior visits to our Service or other websites.
  • Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve personalized ads to you based on your visit to our Service and/or other sites on the Internet.

Your Opt-Out Choices: You have control over the ads you see.

  • You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google's Ads Settings.
  • Alternatively, you can opt out of other third-party vendors' use of cookies for personalized advertising by visiting www.aboutads.info/choices.

If you opt out, you will still see ads, but they will not be personalized based on your browsing history.

10. Children's Privacy

The Service is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new policy on this page. If the changes are significant, we may provide a more prominent notice within the application.
